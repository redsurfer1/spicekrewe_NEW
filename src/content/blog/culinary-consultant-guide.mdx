# What is a research chef and what do they do? When to hire a culinary consultant vs in-house chef

A **research chef** (often aligned with **Research Chefs Association** practice) blends culinary craft with **bench science**—formulation, sensory checkpoints, and documentation that survives QA and co-packer review. A traditional **in-house chef** may own menu execution without owning **spec sheets** or **pilot logs**.

This guide helps you decide **when a consultant wins** versus hiring full-time—and how **marketplaces** compare to **retainer firms**. Ready to scope work? [Hire an on-demand culinary consultant](/hire/culinary-consultant).

## What does a culinary consultant actually deliver?

Typical outputs include **formulas**, **TRDs**, **scaling plans**, **sensory descriptors**, **training guides**, and **vendor-ready documentation**. The mix depends on whether you are launching SKU one or stabilizing line three.

## When to hire a consultant vs building in-house

- **Hire a consultant** when you need surge R&D, clean-label reformulation, or co-packer translation without a $120K+ annual role.
- **Build in-house** when daily production volume requires constant on-line troubleshooting and you can amortize salary across many SKUs.
