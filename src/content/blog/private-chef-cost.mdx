**Private chef** usually means a credentialed cook executing your event on-site—menu, prep, service, and breakdown—with liability aligned to a professional engagement. **Personal chef** often describes recurring meal prep or weekly service; the terms overlap in search, but buyers typically mean **event-based** when they ask “how much for one night.”

## Private chef cost breakdown

- **Per event (dinner for 6–12):** commonly **$800–$3,500+** for labor before groceries—varies with menu complexity, duration, and travel.
- **Per person:** many hosts model **$85–$250+ per guest** all-in when groceries and service are bundled.
- **Per day (multi-meal retreat):** often **$1,200–$4,000+** depending on headcount and service windows.
- **Per year (fractional or quarterly series):** retainer-style programs for brands or family offices—scoped as milestones, not a single dinner.

Expect **$800–$3,500+** for professional labor on a single evening service, before groceries. Add groceries, rentals, and travel as line items in your brief.

### What is the difference between a personal chef and a private chef?

**Private chef** usually signals an **event or on-site experience**. **Personal chef** often means **recurring meal prep**. Searchers use both—define the meal format in your brief to get accurate quotes.
