# How much does a recipe developer cost? Recipe development cost per recipe

Recipe development pricing shifts with scope: a single LTO is not the same investment as a twelve-SKU CPG launch. Food brands usually buy either **hourly R&D time** or a **per-recipe package**—and the right model depends on how many testing rounds, dietary gates, and handoffs you need before production.

### Per-recipe packages ($300–$2,500)

- **Single recipe (restaurant or content):** roughly $300–$800 when styling, photography, and nutritional panels are excluded.
- **Small batch (3–10 recipes):** $1,200–$6,000 depending on documentation depth.
- **CPG launch scale (specs + trials + line validation):** often $8,000–$25,000+ across the program—still cheaper than a mis-run pilot batch.

## What affects the cost?

- **Ingredients and yield targets** (cost-sensitive vs premium positioning)
- **Testing rounds** (one pass vs multi-site validation)
- **Dietary specs** (allergen programs, clean label, claims support)
- **IP ownership** (work-for-hire vs licensed formulations)
- **Exclusivity** (category-exclusive consultants command premium rates)

## Timeline — how long recipe development takes

- **Single recipe (restaurant or editorial):** about **3–7 days** for a tight spec with minimal reformulation.
- **CPG batch programs:** about **4–12 weeks** from brief to line-ready documentation, assuming co-packer alignment and regulatory touchpoints stay on schedule.

## Small brand vs enterprise pricing

Small brands often buy scoped pilots (one hero SKU + documentation). Enterprise teams layer sensory panels, legal review, and multi-plant scale-up—same SK Verified hourly bands, larger total hours. Both paths benefit from a written brief so testing rounds stay intentional, not open-ended.

## Frequently asked questions

### How much does recipe testing cost for a small food brand?

Pilot testing for a small brand typically starts with **20–60 hours** of R&D time at **$150–$190/hr** SK Verified rates, plus ingredient and lab fees if applicable. Package a per-recipe cap in your brief to keep rounds predictable.

### How long does recipe development take?

Plan **3–7 days** for a single controlled recipe, and **4–12 weeks** for a CPG-scale program that includes documentation, trials, and handoff to production.
